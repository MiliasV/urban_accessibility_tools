
# Urban Analytics Tools for Accessibility, Walkability & X-minute Cities

![Status](https://img.shields.io/badge/status-active-success)
![Focus](https://img.shields.io/badge/focus-urban%20accessibility-blue)
![Scope](https://img.shields.io/badge/scope-X--minute%20city-informational)

A curated ecosystem of links to interactive tools and computational methods for analysing **urban accessibility, walkability, and proximity-based planning**.

---

## 🧭 Ecosystem Overview

**accessX** — Python toolkit for network-based accessibility analysis

- **CThood** — Accessibility & equity in X-minute cities  
  - 5- and 15-minute walking catchments  
  - Multi-service accessibility (e.g. food, education, green space)  
  - Distributional and threshold-based equity analysis  

- **CTstreets** — Street-level walkability  
  - Walkability index combining urban form and perception  
  - Open data + participatory expert input  
  - Outputs from street segment to neighbourhood scale  

- **CTwalk** — Co-accessibility & potential encounters  
  - Shared accessibility across population groups  
  - Estimation of reachable populations  
  - Indicators of social cohesion and interaction potential

## 🧪 Methodological Backbone

### accessX — Python Toolkit  
🔗 TBD

A modular Python library for building **reproducible accessibility workflows**.

Core capabilities:
- Network-based isochrones
- H3-based spatial aggregation
- Population data enrichment
- Accessibility and proximity indicators
- Equity indicators

accessX acts as the **analytical engine** supporting the development of multiple interactive tools for accessibility, walkability, and co-accessibility analysis.

## 🌍 Interactive Tools

### CThood — Mapping the X-minute City  
🔗 https://miliasv.github.io/CThood/?city=athens

Interactive platform for exploring **5- and 15-minute accessibility to everyday services**.

- Network-based walksheds using OpenStreetMap pedestrian networks  
- Multi-category accessibility across key urban functions  
- Equity-sensitive indicators to reveal spatial inequalities  
- Designed for comparative analysis across cities  

---

### CTstreets — Street-level Walkability  
🔗 [https://miliasv.github.io/CTstreets/?city=amsterdam](https://miliasv.github.io/CTstreets/?city=amsterdam)

Tool for assessing **walkability through both physical and perceived urban qualities**.

- Street-level walkability index integrating morphology, safety, greenery, and amenities  
- Informed by participatory methods (Q-methodology with practitioners)  
- Multi-scale outputs from street segment to neighbourhood level  
- Highlights intra-urban disparities in walkability  

---

### CTwalk — Co-accessibility & Potential Encounters  
🔗 https://miliasv.github.io/CTwalk/?city=rotterdam

Interactive framework for analysing **shared accessibility to opportunities across populations**.

- Captures joint access rather than individual proximity  
- Estimates how different population groups can reach common destinations  
- Supports analysis of social cohesion and interaction potential  
- Extends accessibility towards relational and social dimensions

## 🧩 Research Scope

This ecosystem supports work on:

- X-minute cities and proximity-based planning  
- Accessibility inequalities and spatial equity  
- Walkability and perceived urban environments  
- Co-accessibility and potential social interactions  
- Data-driven tools for urban policy and design  

Applications include Athens, Amsterdam, Milan, Stockholm, and other European cities.
